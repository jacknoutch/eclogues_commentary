Translations

Despite its age, Fairclough is a much more readable translation than other publicly-available English texts. His version is in prose, close to the Latin, and avoids the archaic vocabulary that usually obscure other translations.

Alpers is a faithful rendering in elegant verse.

  • H. Fairclough (1916) Loeb Translations↗
  • P. Alpers (1977) The singer of the Eclogues Berkeley: Uni. of California Press

Commentaries

  • R. Coleman (1977) Vergil: Eclogues Cambridge: Cambridge Univ. Press
  • W. Clausen (1994) Virgil: Eclogues Oxford: Oxford Univ. Press

Studies

  • M. Putnam (1970) Virgil’s pastoral art: Studies in the Eclogues Princeton, NJ: Princeton Univ. Press.
